2. Intonation and Stress: The Music of English

Beyond individual sounds, how you stress words and vary your pitch (intonation) significantly impacts clarity and meaning in American English.

Word Stress

In multi-syllable words, one syllable is always stressed more than others. This is crucial for natural pronunciation.

Sentence Stress & Rhythm

4. Common Pronunciation Challenges

Many learners face specific challenges with certain sounds. Here are some common ones and how to tackle them.

The American "R" vs. "L"

Distinguishing between the retroflex 'R' and the 'L' (especially dark L) can be tricky.

  • R: Tongue pulls back and up, not touching the roof of the mouth. (e.g., "right", "read")

5. Practice Exercises

Consistent practice is key to improving your accent. Here are some ways to practice:

Listen and Repeat

Listen carefully to native speakers and try to imitate their pronunciation, intonation, and rhythm as closely as possible.

  • Shadowing: Listen to a short audio clip and try to speak along with it, mimicking every detail.
  • Minimal Pairs: Practice words that differ by only one sound (e.g., "ship" vs. "sheep").
